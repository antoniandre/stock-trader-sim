📊 Intraday Trading Bot — Performance Metrics Report

1. Win Rate (don’t obsess over it)

* Percentage of profitable trades
* Intraday bots can be profitable even with 40–55% win rate if risk/reward is strong
* High win rate + tiny profits = often a fragile strategy

⸻

2. Risk–Reward Ratio (R:R)

* Average profit per winning trade vs average loss
* Healthy range: 1.2:1 to 2:1+
* Matters more than win rate long-term

⸻

3. Profit Factor

* Formula: Gross profit ÷ gross loss
* Solid intraday bot: > 1.5
* Strong: 2.0+
* ~1.0 = basically breakeven after fees/slippage

⸻

4. Max Drawdown (“can you sleep at night” metric)

* Largest peak-to-trough loss
* Ideal intraday range: <10–20% (depends on aggressiveness)
* High returns + large drawdowns → often blow up eventually

⸻

5. Sharpe Ratio (risk-adjusted returns)

* Measures return per unit of volatility
* Good: > 1.5
* Strong: 2.0+

⸻

6. Trade Frequency & Consistency

* Number of trades per day
* Look for stable distribution, not random bursts
* Consistency > occasional big wins

⸻

7. Average Holding Time

* Should align with your strategy (seconds / minutes / hours)
* If it drifts → bot may be reacting to noise instead of signal

⸻

8. Slippage & Transaction Costs

* Intraday strategies live or die here
* Track:
    * Expected vs actual fill price
    * Fees impact on net profit
* Many “profitable” bots die after costs

⸻

9. Expectancy (most underrated metric)

* Average profit per trade over time
* Positive expectancy = real edge

⸻

10. Equity Curve Smoothness

* Aim for steady upward slope, not jagged spikes
* Smooth = repeatable edge
* Spiky = luck or overfitting

⸻

⚡ Quick Reality Check

A “good” intraday bot typically looks like:

* Profit Factor: 1.5 – 2.5
* Win Rate: 45–60%
* Max Drawdown: <15%
* Sharpe Ratio: ~1.5 – 2+
* Returns: consistent daily/weekly gains (not lottery spikes)

⸻

🧠 Core Performance Metrics (Stocks Intraday)

1. Profit Factor (non-negotiable)

* Bare minimum: 1.5
* Realistically viable after costs: 1.7 – 2.5
* Stocks have spreads + commissions → anything lower gets eaten alive

⸻

2. Expectancy (your true edge)

* Must be positive and stable per trade
* For stocks: even $0.02–$0.10 per share can be solid (depending on volume)
* If highly unstable → regime-dependent (dangerous)

⸻

3. Win Rate + R:R (together, not separate)

A. Momentum / Breakout Bot

* Win rate: 40–50%
* R:R: 1.8:1 – 3:1

B. Mean Reversion Bot (partially visible but implied)

* Win rate: ~55–70%
* Lower R:R (typically)

⸻

🧩 Big Picture Insight

If you compress everything into one idea:

👉 Expectancy + Cost Control + Drawdown discipline = survival
👉 Profit factor + smooth equity curve = real edge

Most bots fail not because of signal…
…but because of costs, overfitting, or poor risk control.
